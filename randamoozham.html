Randamoozham

Author: M. T. Vasudevan Nair

Genre: Mythology, drama, historical fiction

Publication date: December, 1984

“The novel presents Bhima as a deeply human character, highlighting his emotional turmoil, unspoken love for Draupadi, and strained relationship with his brothers. Stripped of the divine and heroic gloss, the epic’s events are portrayed through Bhima’s practical and grounded perspective, revealing the harsh realities of war, power, and duty. His loyalty to his family is juxtaposed with his frustration at being constantly relegated to the backgrounddespite his significant contributions. The novel ultimately offers a nuanced exploration of Bhima's inner world, making him the reluctant, yet heroic, heart of the Mahabharata.”
